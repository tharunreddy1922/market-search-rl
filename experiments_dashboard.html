Key Findings — Full Training Run (April 2026)

Double-DQN (+518.54) and DQN (+516.08) rank 1st and 2nd in the baseline, both achieving ~99.7% completion and 18% price discounts. In experiments, Double-DQN leads in Exp1 and Exp3; DQN leads in Exp2.
Dueling-DQN (+456.09) scored lower than DQN and Double-DQN in this run, achieving only 96.73% completion. Its conservative Q-value estimates cause earlier episode termination compared to the other DQN variants.
PPO (+391.66) outperformed all tabular agents at the full 10,000-episode budget, confirming that with sufficient training it learns a competent policy — but still achieves only −12.19% price discount versus −18% for DQN family.
Exp 2 (10 goods/store) is the hardest: PPO collapses to +89.0 (83.3% completion), and for the first time DQN (+406.2) is competitive with but surpassed by Q-Learning (+361.8) and approached by Greedy (+315.6).
Exp 3 (80% availability) confirms the mechanism: Q-Learning rises from +396 to +484, nearly matching DQN (+534). Higher availability reduces the diversity requirement for price-routing discovery, closing the neural advantage gap.

Baseline Analysis

Double-DQN (+518.54, std 41.11) edges DQN (+516.08, std 36.82) — both achieve ~99.7% completion and −18% price discount. The lower std of DQN suggests more stable episode-to-episode performance.
Dueling-DQN (+456.09, 96.73% completion) is the weakest DQN variant in this run. Its value-advantage decomposition produces more conservative Q-values, triggering earlier End actions and lower completion.
PPO (+391.66, 97.60%) outperforms all tabular and heuristic agents except Best-Deal at the full 10,000-episode budget — a stronger result than earlier 3,000-episode pilots showed.
Expected-SARSA (+400.86) is the strongest tabular agent, beating Q-Learning (+396.74) and SARSA (+373.10). Its variance-reduced updates produce more stable Q-value estimates in the stochastic environment.
Best-Deal heuristic (+338.47) is the most competitive no-training agent, visiting only 2.05 stores on average — matching DQN-family routing efficiency through its price-weighted scoring formula.
TimeAware leads on brand preference (4.49/5) and expiry (4.83 days) — the only agent optimising quality over reward. Prof-Heuristic achieves the freshest purchases (1.51 days expiry) by its strict dual-threshold rule.

Exp 0 Analysis

DQN (+491.9) and Double-DQN (+482.0) maintain strong performance even at the reduced 3,000-episode budget, achieving ~98.5-98.8% completion and −17% discounts — demonstrating efficient off-policy learning.
PPO (+342.8) nearly matches Q-Learning (+340.4) at this reduced budget. Both are mid-table, well behind the DQN family but ahead of heuristics. This confirms PPO's sample efficiency is competitive with tabular methods.
The price discount gap is clear: DQN achieves −17.3%, PPO only −10.9%. The price-routing correlation requires diverse experience that DQN's replay buffer provides but PPO's short rollouts do not.
Exp 1: 3-Hour Duration + Store Revisiting
Change: TOTAL_DURATION 120 → 180 minutes. Store revisiting allowed — subsequent visits cost travel time only (5 min, no re-entry charge).
Key result: Double-DQN (+504.7) leads. DQN drops to +476.1. PPO struggles most (+264.5).

Exp 1 Analysis

Double-DQN (+504.7) improves over baseline (+482.0) by exploiting revisiting — it can circle back to low-premium stores, achieving −18.2% discount. DQN drops slightly (+476.1 vs +491.9) as the longer horizon increases episode variance.
PPO collapses from +342.8 to +264.5. On-policy rollouts of 256 steps rarely encounter the specific revisiting sequences needed for the extended environment, leaving the advantage signal sparse.
Q-Learning falls from +340.4 to +329.9. Its 20-minute time-bin state hash is less well-calibrated for the 180-minute horizon, reducing generalisation quality.
Heuristics are unchanged — Greedy (+302.6) and TimeAware (+255.2) are deterministic and do not benefit from the longer budget, as they always exhaust their strategy within the first 120 minutes anyway.
Exp 2: Restricted Coverage — 10 Goods per Store
Change: Each store stocks only 10 of 20 goods (3 brands each), with deliberate partial overlap ensuring all goods reachable.
Key result: DQN (+406.2) leads RL. Greedy (+315.6) becomes competitive. PPO collapses to +89.0 (83.3% completion).

Exp 2 Analysis

DQN (+406.2) is the strongest RL agent in Exp 2, slightly ahead of Double-DQN (+372.1). Under restricted coverage, DQN's broader exploration discovers multi-store routing patterns more reliably.
Greedy (+315.6) achieves 100% completion and is now the closest competitor to DQN. Its coverage-maximising routing rule — visit the store with highest aggregate quality for remaining goods — aligns perfectly with the specialised structure.
PPO collapses to +89.0 and only 83.3% completion. On-policy rollouts rarely encounter the specific multi-store sequences needed for coverage. The terminal missing-item penalty is too sparse to guide learning effectively.
Q-Learning (+361.8, 94.4% completion) is surprisingly competitive in Exp 2. Its coarser state representation generalises better across the reduced goods-per-store configuration than expected.
Exp 3: Higher Availability — 80% Probability
Change: AVAIL_PROB 0.60 → 0.80. All other settings identical to baseline.
Key result: Double-DQN (+537.2) and DQN (+534.0) lead. Q-Learning (+484.8) nearly closes the gap. PPO improves dramatically (+429.9).

Exp 3 Analysis

All agents improve. Double-DQN (+537.2) and DQN (+534.0) both exceed their baseline performance. DQN achieves −18.8% discount — the best price performance across all experiments.
Q-Learning rises from +396.74 to +484.8, the largest absolute gain in the study. With less availability uncertainty, the tabular agent's coarser state representation becomes adequate and it generalises well across episodes.
PPO improves dramatically — from +391.66 at baseline (10k episodes) equivalent to +429.9 here, confirming that higher availability reduces the experience-diversity requirement for price-routing discovery.
TimeAware achieves the highest brand preference score across all experiments (4.57/5) under 80% availability — abundant preferred brands plus adaptive quality thresholds produces near-optimal quality purchasing.
